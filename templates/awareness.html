Guidelines for a Healthier Community

Small daily actions lead to massive community transformations. Explore our guidelines below to see how you can contribute to a healthier neighborhood.

🗑️ Smart Waste Management

  • Segregate at Source: Always use separate bins for wet (biodegradable) and dry (recyclable) waste.
  • Say No to Single-Use Plastics: Carry reusable bags and avoid plastic bottles to reduce non-biodegradable pollution.
  • Proper Disposal: Never dump garbage in empty plots or open streets. Always use designated municipal bins.

💧 Water & Drainage Care

  • Protect the Drains: Do not throw solid waste, oils, or plastics into open drains to prevent clogging and flooding.
  • Prevent Stagnation: Empty containers, tires, and pots that collect rainwater to stop mosquitoes from breeding.

🙌 Public & Personal Hygiene

  • Handwashing: Wash hands regularly with soap, especially before meals and after handling waste.
  • Community Drives: Actively participate in local cleanliness drives and educate your neighbors about sanitation.
